1. ON THE TRANSFORMATION OF LIGHT INTO HEAT IN SOLIDS - J, FRENKEL
* TOPICS - EXCITONS, THEORY
* https://journals.aps.org/pr/pdf/10.1103/PhysRev.37.17
significance - first theoretical article discussing excitons.

coined the term frenkel excitons:
bound electron-hole pairs with spatial dimensions smaller than a single unit cell.
associated with binding energies of ~0.1-1eV.

2. The Structure of Electronic Excitation Levels in Insulating Crystals - Gregory H. Wannier
* TOPIC - EXCITONS, THEORY
* https://journals.aps.org/pr/pdf/10.1103/PhysRev.52.191
significance - first theoretical aticle discussing wannier excitons - weakly bound electron hole pairs.

coined the term wannier excitons:
bound electron-hole pairs with spatial dimensions spanning multiple unit cells.
associated with binding energies <0.1eV.

3. Direct measurement of key exciton properties: energy, dynamics and spatial distribution of the wave function - Shuo Dong et al.
* TOPIC - EXCITONS, ARPES, MEASUREMENT
* https://arxiv.org/pdf/2012.15328
* WSe2
significance - time resolved ARPES measurement claiming to observe excitons, by binding energy

shows excited electrons population ~100meV below an energy band when pumping with particular wavelength.
(when pumping with one wavelength a certain energy band is at a certain energy, and when pumping with another one, it is 100meV lower).

notes:
they are using a 35fs pulse, suggesting an enegry resolution of ~117meV, and claiming to seeing excitons based on a difference of 100meV - lower than the resolution.
also, they are saying that the band appears lower, at 1.66eV (above fermi) when pumping with 1.55eV, compared to the band showing at 1.76eV when pumping with 3.1eV.
however, the center of the band could appear lower simply due to the the electrons populating lower energy states from the ones available.


4. Optical Spectrum of Excitons in the Crystal Lattice. - E. F. Gross
* TOPIC - EXCITONS, MEASUREMENT, ABSORPTION
* https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/BF02746069
* CuO2
significance - first experimental observation of excitons.

hydrogen-like multiple absorption lines beyond a simple transition, suggesting a class of bands of a bound particle.
